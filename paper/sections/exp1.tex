\section{Experiment 1: how local can wiring search get?}
\label{sec:exp1}

\paragraph{Protocol.} MLPs with two hidden layers of width 1024, weights
frozen at a signed-constant initialization \citep{ramanujan2020whats}, mask
keeping $50\%$ of connections per layer. Controls: \textsc{random} (fixed
random mask), \textsc{readout} (backprop trains the final linear layer only,
on frozen random features), and \textsc{dense} (ordinary BP on all weights,
no mask). SGD with momentum $0.9$ and cosine decay; 30 epochs (MNIST) / 40
epochs (CIFAR-10); batch size 128; 3 seeds. Learning rates: \ep{} and
\textsc{dense} $0.1$, \dfa{} $0.05$, \rmhebb{} $0.005$.

\label{tab:exp1}

\toprule
Rule & Signal to hidden layers & MNIST & CIFAR-10 \\
\midrule
\textsc{dense} (control) & full gradient (weights train) & $98.7 \pm 0.1$ & $53.0 \pm 0.4$ \\
\ep{} & full gradient & $98.2 \pm 0.1$ & $\mathbf{55.9 \pm 0.2}$ \\
\dfa{} & random projection of error vector & $96.9 \pm 0.1$ & $53.3 \pm 0.2$ \\
\textsc{readout} (control) & none (readout only) & $94.9 \pm 0.1$ & $45.6 \pm 0.2$ \\
\rmhebb{} & one global scalar per sample & $51.7 \pm 3.9$ & $26.3 \pm 6.3$ \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\paragraph{Results.} Table~\ref{tab:exp1} and Figure~\ref{fig:exp1curves}
show a graded degradation along the locality axis. Three observations. \textbf{(i) Wiring matches, then
beats, weights.} On MNIST, gradient mask search is within half a point of
dense training. On CIFAR-10---where a dense MLP overfits---mask search over
the same frozen random weights \emph{outperforms} dense training by
$2.9$ points. Restricting learning to connectivity acts as a strong
regularizer while retaining enough capacity, strengthening the claim that
the random network already contains the solution
\citep{malach2020proving}. \textbf{(ii) A broadcast error vector is enough.}
\dfa{} mask search---no weight transport, no chained backward---lands within
$1.3$ (MNIST) and $2.6$ (CIFAR-10) points of \ep{}, beats the trained-readout
control by $2.0$ and $7.7$ points, and \emph{matches dense weight training}
on CIFAR-10. Local wiring rules genuinely restructure hidden layers.
\textbf{(iii) A scalar is not enough.} \rmhebb{} learns---$4{\times}$ chance
on MNIST, $2.6{\times}$ on CIFAR-10---but plateaus far below every
vector-signal rule, with high seed variance. In this setting the information
threshold for effective connectivity search lies between a broadcast vector
and a broadcast scalar.

\centering

\caption{Mask-budget robustness on MNIST: final accuracy vs.\ fraction of

\paragraph{Sparsity.} Sweeping the kept fraction over
$\{70\%, 50\%, 30\%, 10\%\}$ on MNIST (Figure~\ref{fig:sparsity}) changes
\ep{} accuracy only from $98.2$ to $96.9$ and \dfa{} from $96.9$ to $94.8$:
even keeping one tenth of random connections, the local rule still beats the
trained readout on frozen features. Connectivity search is robust to the mask
budget.

\subsection{Two failure modes worth documenting}
\label{sec:exp1-stability}

\paragraph{The sign factor in straight-through mask gradients.} Selecting the
top-$k$ mask by $|s|$ while passing the straight-through gradient to $s$
directly drops the $\partial|s|/\partial s = \operatorname{sign}(s)$ factor;
every negative-score synapse then moves in the wrong direction, and learning
sits at chance. Applying $|\cdot|$ \emph{before} the straight-through
operator (so autodiff supplies the sign), or equivalently multiplying manual
updates by $\operatorname{sign}(s)$, restores learning entirely. We flag this
because the failure is silent: losses hover near initialization and no error
is raised.

\paragraph{Hebbian runaway and its stabilization.} A naive three-factor rule
diverges here: with ReLU activity the eligibility $a_i x_j$ is non-negative,
so reinforced co-activations grow activations, which grow eligibilities, until
logits saturate (we observed logit magnitudes ${>}10^2$ within 200 steps),
the softmax collapses to one-hot, exploration dies, and accuracy pins at
chance. Three standard components stabilize it: batch-centered (covariance)
eligibilities, RMS-normalized per-layer updates so the learning rate sets the
score step scale, and a loss-based rather than sampled-action reward. The
stabilized rule learns but remains far below the vector-signal rules,
suggesting the gap is informational, not merely a tuning artifact.
